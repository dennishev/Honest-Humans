Creed II

WHAT ARE YOU FIGHTING FOR? Recently I went to see Creed 2. It wasn’t the greatest movie I’ve ever seen, but something strange did happen to me while watching. I had the most intense emotional response I’ve ever had while watching a movie. I was crying and feeling an intense sense of attachment to what was going on. And tbh, it’s really hard to get me to cry. It was as if the my life and my inner world, that I hadn’t been able to really out into words was being played out on this massive screen. It was telling a compelling story, but it was telling me my story. I figured out three themes that really popped out to me, and helped me, better understand my own life. Why are you fighting. What is your name? Create your own legacy. This little article is only going to talk about the first one. As a young adult, you are faced with your future and the pressure, to somehow figure out how to meet your own expectations. You’re possibly in the biggest fight of your life. For longest time, I was fighting. Choosing specific things because of some unconscious reason, that I could vaguely tell you, but there was something deeper. In the movie Rocky asks Creed, “Why are you fighting?” And he gives him some vague answer and seems unsure. This was a repetitive theme throughout the film and I felt like I was being asked the same question. I realized, that I wasn’t just fighting to have time and financial freedom, but I’m fighting to prove something to myself. To really dig deep in life, not for something as simple as a better paycheck, or more relaxed weekend, but to become victorious over my fears. I’m fighting to be better, not better than other people. Better than myself. If you haven’t asked yourself this question, maybe it’s because you haven’t realized that you are even in a fight. Maybe you’ve given the battle up. Maybe you have decided to allow complacency to rule your life. In that Theatre, God was asking me a question, “Why are you fighting?” To which I really I had to search my soul to find out the answer. I want you to find your answer because finding mine, has helped me become more intentional and understanding of my own life pursuits. It’s fueled my flame, and empowered me to fight harder than I ever have before. Why are you in this? Sometimes our “why” is fueled by our insecurities, and truth be told, it won’t last us through the fight of life. We need a why that’s bigger than ourselves. Why are you alive? WHAT IS YOUR NAME? Who are you? Often times, your last name fills you with pride. It isn’t just a set of vowels, consonants and syllables; it’s your family’s mantra unexpressed. When I think of my name (Josh King) I think of bravery and grit. It gives me identity. The moment I was born, I was a King. The thing is, my parents and the people before them were the ones who created that identity, that mantra and that name. I get to live up to that name, and push the envelope a little farther to even tally pass the baton along to the next generation of Kings. In the Movie, Creed is asked by a referee, “What is your name?” Creed replies, with, “Creed!” And in that seemingly routine moment, the whole audience knew that the man in that ring, wasn’t just questioning his name, He was asking the bigger question we all ask ourselves; he was asking Creed, “who are you?” Creed answered with a surge of intense emotion. He was answering for his father, his mother, his wife, and his baby. He was reminding himself of what being a Creed meant. All the history attached to the name, all the emotions attached to that name. Your name means more than we think Figure out your name. CREATE YOUR OWN LEGACY! Finding your purpose, and making it your own. We all need something to fight for, and we all should leave our signatures on the geography of our family’s map. In Creed 2, there’s a subliminal repetitive theme carried throughout the film. It’s this idea that Creed needs to build his own legacy, that he is somehow standing in the shadow of his father’s unfinished work. I understood that the reason Creed had to create his own legacy, and the reason he was in his father’s shadow, wasn’t because of a family failure(if you don’t follow the movie series, Creed’s father was killed in the boxing ring) the reason Creed was in his father’s shadow, was because He never picked up where his father left off. He really just ran the same race that his father did, but not as far. He hadn’t picked the baton up and carried it farther. This movie was revealing something inside of me that I didn’t even know was there. One of the reasons of why I’m fighting, is because I need to create my own legacy. I need to pick up the baton where it was left off and bring in farther and carry it in a different way. Create your own legacy.

Jumpstart

If you have an area of your life where you are lacking find a jump starter Find a person who embodies the epitome of the area that you are lacking and get around them; their strength will rub off on you and jumpstart that once vacant and dead area of your life. I work at a nonprofit. A lot of volunteers work with me and many of them have mental disabilities, and although, they’re not the best at stacking bunch of boxes in the most strategic way, theses people have become my friends. I really have come to enjoy my work days because of one person in particular. He is the funniest guy ever!! I have recently gone through a season where I’ve been more serious and somber. I’m a young adult, recently married, and a baby on the way. I’ve got a lot on my plate, mostly because I like it that way, but I can become more serious as I take my future extremely serious. I noticed I was becoming boring. I wasn’t having as much fun. I was losing that wonder of life that comes with being young and inspired. What I noticed was that, whenever I hang out with this guy, Because he is so goofy, It rubs off on me, so much, that I become goofy, even after he is gone. This guy, has jump started an area of my life that was growing wildly weaker and weaker. He has re-lit the fire inside of my goofy side. So now I wonder, whenever I have an area of my life, where I’m lacking I can find someone who excels in that area, and it will naturally rub off on me, just by being around them. Community makes a little more sense now. YOU HAVE TO BE OK WITH NOT BEING THE BEST IN EVERY AREA OF YOUR LIFE ALL THE TIME. Raise your hand if you’re a young adult. If you happen to be young, and also entering that infamous stage of adulthood, then you’ve probably encountered some type of identity trials. This season has been trying. Not because it’s emotionally taxing, but because I’ve been confused about some things. Is it ok for me not to be excelling in every single area of my life? If it’s not, then how am I supposed to go after all that I am passionate about? I have so many things that I really want to pursue and I am doing them, but other things that I have previously given a lot of life to, are in many ways taking a backseat. Since I’m new at pursuing multiple areas and attempting at thriving in all of them, I often question if it’s ok. There’s a lot to learn. Let’s say you’re interested in becoming an entrepreneur or a real estate agent, or any sort of semi-promising career paths. These are all going to take a lot of time and energy to become great in. And Greatness is not an expendable attribute in my book. Naturally though, other areas are going to feel lack. I heard Steven Furtick talking about this exact thing, but in the context of TD Jakes, giving him advice. TD jakes told him, you’re just going to have to accept that part of life. It takes time and energy to get things going. You do your best to be balanced and manage it. This gave me so much peace and reassurement that it’s ok. That maybe I’m doing this thing right after all. Please tell me you can relate!!!
